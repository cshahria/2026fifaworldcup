## Executive Summary: VTA Light Rail Parking & Commute Strategy

This revised strategy removes **Old Ironsides** due to the hard security perimeter restrictions during the World Cup matches. To optimize the commute for Fan Operations, transit plans must adapt to strict station locations, real-world station counts, and precise parking rules.

### Key Insights

* **Station with the Least Number of Stops (Active):** **Lick Mill Station** (Eastbound from Mountain View) and **Champion Station** (Westbound from Alum Rock) are physically closest to the stadium property footprint. However, **Great America Station** remains the mandatory, direct event drop-off point. Looking strictly at viable park-and-ride options, **River Oaks** sits just 4 stops away from the stadium line.
* **Station with Most Historical Parking:** **Capitol Station** (951 spaces) and **Ohlone/Chynoweth** (549 spaces) in South San Jose offer the largest parking footprints system-wide to guarantee a spot on high-volume match days.
* **Milpitas Transit Center Fee Note:** The parking facility located at **1755 S. Milpitas Blvd.** is an intermodal hub shared with BART. Because it is a commuter garage structure, **it charges a $3 daily parking fee** managed via the Passport Transit app. This fee is mandatory for all vehicles parked in the structure on game days.
