💬 FAQ for Family & Friends

The easiest option is VTA Light Rail — take the Mountain View or Winchester line to the Levi's Stadium stop. From San Francisco, take Caltrain to Santa Clara station, then connect to VTA. From the East Bay, take BART to Fremont or Milpitas and transfer to VTA. On match days, transit services typically run extended and more frequent services. Check the VTA and Caltrain websites for match day timetables.
Yes, but parking fills quickly and advance purchase is strongly recommended. Lots open several hours before kickoff. Rideshare (Uber/Lyft) drop-off zones are available. Given traffic after matches, public transit is often the faster option for getting home.
Bags must be clear and no larger than 12" x 6" x 12". Small clutches up to 4.5" x 6.5" are allowed without being clear. No outside food or drinks, no large umbrellas, no selfie sticks, no professional cameras with detachable lenses. Always check the official Levi's Stadium bag policy before your visit as rules may be updated closer to the tournament.
The Bay Area Host Committee is organizing official Fan Zones and public viewing events across the region. Check bayareahostcommittee.com for locations and schedules. In the US, matches are broadcast on Fox, FS1, Telemundo, and Peacock depending on the game.
Fan Operations volunteers help fans navigate the stadium — finding their seats, answering questions, making wayfinding announcements, and keeping the energy positive. Shifts typically start 2–3 hours before kickoff. It's one of the most guest-facing volunteer roles at the tournament.
Just bookmark this site — cshahria.github.io/2026fifaworldcup — and check back after each game day. A new entry gets added after each shift with photos and a write-up. No app, no login needed.
